About Me

I've been passionate about programming since I was 12 and have worked professionally as a software engineer for over 25 years. I've always loved solving puzzles, and for me, programming has always felt like that -- challenging, inventive, and deeply satisfying. When people think of creative disciplines, they usually picture musicians, painters, or writers -- not programmers. But coding demands as much imagination as technical skill. The puzzles I enjoy solving most are the ones that result in code that's elegant, efficient, and enduring.


After a period of personal exploration and skill renewal -- including earning professional certifications and developing a new portfolio app -- I'm excited to return to the field with fresh perspective and renewed focus.

CrossCracker

A prototype crossword puzzle solver.

CrossCracker (play demo video)

CrossCracker is a prototype crossword puzzle solving app.

  • Uses AI to solve crossword puzzles. No, really!
  • Currently uses the Llama 3.1 (8b) model running on Ollama. I tried a bunch of different models, and that one was the best compromise between size, speed, and effectiveness. (The DeepSeek model that caused such an uproar in the news was total garbage, BTW.)
  • The code required to integrate an AI model into an app is trivial. The tricky part is getting the "prompt" just right. Unlike any other coding API, your app interacts with an AI using natural language, just like when you use ChatGPT's web interface. But for its responses to be useful to your app, you have to "convince" it to respond in a very specific, structured way, and it doesn't always listen. It's really more like training a dog than writing code!
  • In terms of the coding, the algorithm for selecting which clues to ask and which answers to use was orders of magnitude more difficult than integrating the AI. Trickier still was deciding when an answer that was previously placed was wrong and should be removed. I'm still improving that, and probably will be forever.
  • Unfortunatly, it's too expensive to put an AI-enabled app on the Internet just for personal use. But you can see the video of it running here, and the code is available on my GitHub (see the link to the left).
